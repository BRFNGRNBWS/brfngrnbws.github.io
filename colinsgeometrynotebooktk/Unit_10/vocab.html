1. Cube: A three dimensional solid object bounded by six square faces
  2. Polyhedron: A solid figure with many plane faces
  3. Prism: A solid geometric figure whose two end faces are similar, equal, and parallel rectilinear figures
  4. Lateral Faces: Faces in a prism or pyramid that aren't the base
  5. Lateral Edges: Edges that connect lateral faces
  6. Right Prism: Geometric solid that has a polygon as its base and vertical sides perpendicular to the base
  7. Oblique Prism: A prism in which the bases are parallel, but one is not directly above the other
  8. Lateral Area: The surface area of the lateral faces in a prism
  9. Total Area: The surface area of all faces of a prism
  10. Pyramid: A polyhedron formed by connecting a polygonal base and a point, which forms a triangle with each of the base edges
  11. Regular Pyramid: A pyramid whose base is a regular polygona and whose lateral sides are all equal in length
  12. Cylinder: A solid figure formed by two circles and one curved side
  13. Oblique Cylinder: A cylinder with two parallel circles who aren't directly on top of one another
  14. Cavalierri's Principle: A method of finding the volume of any solid for which cross-sections by parallel planes have equal areas. Formula: Volume = Bh, where B is the area of the cross-section and h is the height of the solid
  15. Cone: A three-dimensional geomtric shape that tapers smoothly from a flat base to a point
  16. Slant Height: The distance along a lateral face from the base to the apex along the center of the face
  17. Sphere: A geometric figure that is perfectly round and circular, like a ball
  18. Similar Solids: Two solids whose ratios of corresponsing sides are equal
  19. Area: The measure of the amount of space in a 2-d object
  20. Perimeter: The total length of all the sides of a figure
  21. Circumfrence: The distance around the circle, or a circle's perimeter
  22. Volume: The measure of the amount of space in a 3-d object
  23. Apothem: A line from the center of a regular polygon at right angles to any of its sides
