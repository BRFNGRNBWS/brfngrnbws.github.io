• Isosceles Triangle Theorem: If two sides of a triangle are congruent, then the angles opposite those sides are congruent.
  • Converse of the Isosceles Triangle Theorem: If two angles of a triangle are congruent, then the sides opposite those angles are congruent.
  • 4.4.3: The measure of each angle of an equalateral triangle is 60°
  • 4.4.4: The bisector of the vertex angle of an isosceles triangle is the perpendicular bisector fo the base.
  • Exterior Angle Theorem: The measure of an exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the measures of the angles of the remote interior angles.
  • Overlapping Angles Theorem: Given ∠AOD with points B and C in its interior, the following statements are true:
    • If m∠AOB = m∠COD, then m∠AOC = m∠BOD
    • If m∠AOC = m∠BOD, then m∠AOB = m∠COD
  • Jurgensen Isosceles Triangle Theorem: If two sides of a triangle are congruent, then the angles opposite those sides are congruent.
    • Corollary 1: An equalateral triangle is also equiangular.
    • Corollary 2: An equilateral triangle had three 60° angles.
    • Corollary 3: The bisector of the vertex angle of an isosceles triangle is perpendicular to the base at its midpoint.
  • 4-2: If two angles of a triangle are congruent, then the sides opposite those angles are congruent
    • Corollary: An equalateral triangle is also equalateral.
  • Angle Addition Theorem: If point B lies in the interior of ∠AOC, then m∠AOC + m∠BOC = m∠AOC. If ∠AOC is a straight angle and B is any point not on line AC, then m∠AOB + m∠BOC = 180.
  • Angle Addition Postulate: If B is between A and C, then AB + BC = AC.
