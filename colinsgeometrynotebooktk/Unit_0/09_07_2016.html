• Equalateral Triangle: A type of triangle with all sides equal in length
  • Isosceles Triangle: A type of triangle with at least two sides of equal length
  • Quadrilateral: A polygon with four edges and four verticies
  • Regular Polygon: A polygon in which all sides and verticies are congruent
  • Trapezoid: A type of quadrilateral with exacly one pair of parallel sides
  • Polygon: A two-dimensional shape with at least three straight sides and angles
  • Parallelogram: A four-sided polygon with one set of parallel sides
  • Rectangle: A plane figure with four straight sides and four right angles
  • Rhombus: A type of parallelogram with a pair of opposite and equal acute angles, and a pair of opposite and equal obtuse angles
  • Kite: A quadrilateral whose four sides can be grouped into two pairs of equal-length sides that are adjacent to each other
  • Square: A polygon with four equal sides and four right angles
  • Pentagon: A polygon with five straight sides and five verticies
  • Hexagon: A polygon with six straight sides and six verticies
