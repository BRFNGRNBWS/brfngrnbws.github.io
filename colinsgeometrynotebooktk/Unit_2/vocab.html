1. Transformation: A process that changes a shape or set of points in some way, while keeping the shape similar to the original
  2. Rigid Motion: A way of moving points so that the distance between them stays the same
  3. Isometry: A transformation that preserves the distance between all points
  4. Translation: A type of transformation that moves an object a certain distance, without being altered in any way
  5. Rotation: A type of transformation that rotates an object around a center point, without modifying it in any other way
  6. Reflection: A type of transformation where an object's points are made to be the same distance from a line of reflection
  7. Dilation: A transformation that makes an image that is similar to the original shape
  8. Pre-Image: The original shape in a transformation
  9. Image: The final shape in a transformation
  10. Rectangle: A quadrilateral with four right angles
  11. Parallelogram: A quadrilateral with two pairs of parallel sides
  12. Trapezoid: A quadrilateral that has at least one pair of parallel sides
  13. Kite: A quadrilateral with two pairs of equal length sides, where the two sides in the pair are adjacent to each other
  14. Regular Polygon: A polygon that has all congruent angle measures and side lengths
  15. Orientation: The rotational position of an object on a plane
  16. Symmetry: An object having symmetry means it can be fitted onto itself, either by rotation or folding the shape in half
  17. Rotational Symmetry: An object is able to be rotated around a center point and become congruent to itself at some point
  18. Reflectional Symmetry: The object can be folded in half, and both halves will be congruent
  19. Tessellation: The tiling of a plane using geometric shapes, with no gaps or overlaps
  20. Glide Reflection: A combination of a reflection over a line, and then a translation parllel to that line
  21. Composition of Functions: Applying one function to another
  22. Composition of Transformations: A combination of two or more transformations
