1. Point: A location in space
  2. Line: A straight, one dimensional figure that extends infinitely in both directions
  3. Plane: A flat, two dimensional figure that extends infinitely
  4. Line Segment: A part of a line that has two distinct end points
  5. Ray: A line with a single endpoint that extends infinitely in one direction
  6. Angle: A figure formed by two rays, sharing a common endpoint
  7. Circle: The set of all points on a plane that are equadistant from one point
  8. Perpendicular Lines: Two lines that form a 90 degree angle where they meet
  9. Parallel Lines: Two lines that never meet, on a two dimesional plane
  10. Distance Along a Line: The length of a segment of a line between two points
  11. Distance Around a Circular Arc: The length of a portion of the circumfrence of a circle
  12. Midpoint Formula: A formula used to find the point exactly in the middle of two other points
  13. Distance Formula: A formula used to find the exact distance between two points
  14. Congruent: Equal in size and shape
  15. Vertical Angles: A pair of non-adjacent angles formed when two lines intersect
  16. Transversal: A line that passes through two lines at two distinct points
  17. Alternate Interior Angles: When two lines are crossed by a transversal, the angles on opposite sides of the transversal, but inside the two lines
  18. Alternate Exterior Angles: When two lines are crossed by a transversal, the angles on opposite sides of the transversal, but outside the two lines
  19. Corresponding Angles: Two angles formed in the same place on both lines crossd by a transversal
  20. Polygon: A closed plane figure with at least three straight sides and angles
  21. Triangle: A closed plane figure with 3 straight sides and angles
  22. Quadrilateral: A closed plane figure with 4 straight sides and angles
  23. Pentagon: A 5-sided polygon
